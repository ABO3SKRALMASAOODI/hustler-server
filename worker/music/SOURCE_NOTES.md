# Music licence record — Valmera background music library

All 24 tracks below are **CC0 1.0 Universal (public-domain dedication)**, sourced from the
Free Music Archive. CC0 permits commercial use, modification and redistribution with **no
attribution requirement**, so nothing here creates an obligation inside a customer's exported video.

## How each licence was verified

Every track was checked individually, twice, against the track's own page on freemusicarchive.org:

1. Candidates came only from FMA's advanced search with `music-filter-public-domain=1` and
   `only-instrumental=1` (an FMA-enforced filter, not free-text uploader metadata).
2. For each track, `https://freemusicarchive.org/track/<handle>/downloadOverlay/` was fetched and the
   machine-readable `<a rel="license">` element parsed. A track was kept **only** if that element
   pointed at `https://creativecommons.org/publicdomain/zero/1.0/`.
3. Any track whose page also referenced a restrictive `creativecommons.org/licenses/*` URL
   (BY, BY-SA, NC) was rejected outright.
4. After download, the licence was re-read from the live page a second time as an independent check.
   All 24 passed both reads.

Audio was obtained from FMA's public `/stream/` endpoint, which serves the full-length file
anonymously. No authentication was bypassed at any point.

## Caveats worth knowing

- **`bpm` is `null` for every track.** FMA does not publish tempo, and it was not estimated or guessed.
- **"Instrumental" is FMA's uploader-declared flag.** It was not verified by listening. The flag is
  under-inclusive rather than over-inclusive (artists tag fewer tracks instrumental than actually are),
  so false positives are unlikely — but spot-check before shipping if a vocal would be fatal.
- **Mood labels are our own editorial judgement**, inferred from the album each track belongs to
  (e.g. HoliznaCC0's `public-domain-lofi` → chill). They are not stated by the source.
- CC0 is a dedication by the uploading artist. For these artists the works are their own originals,
  so the dedication covers both composition and recording — unlike public-domain *historical*
  recordings, where composition and recording expire separately. No PD-by-age recordings were used,
  deliberately, to avoid that split-rights problem.
